Find Your Skin Type

Several criteria are used to classify the different types of skin. For example, Fitzpatrick's classification, first described in 1975, is based on skin color and its response to sun exposure. It is used to determine the proper type of sun protection factor or to predict the risk of skin cancer, among others. However, from a cosmetic point of view, skin is classified according to several factors related to its balance: sebaceous secretion, hydration and sensitivity level. Thus, each type of skin will have its own characteristics and require different cares. The type of skin is determined by genetics, although it will also be affected by other factors and can change with time. Based on these characteristics, there are five types of healthy skin: normal, dry, oily, combination (both oily and dry skin) and sensitive. Below, we describe the main characteristics of each type of skin.

Overview of Skin Types

Skin types describe how the skin behaves and how much oil it produces. The main types are normal, dry, oily, combination, and sensitive. Each type has unique characteristics and needs. Understanding your skin type helps you choose the right products and create a suitable skincare routine.

Objective of us

The Skin Type section aims to help users quickly understand their skin type and choose suitable products. It provides simple explanations of each skin type, guiding users toward better skincare decisions. This section improves user confidence, supports informed product selection, and makes the website easier to navigate

Finding Your Skin Type

Skin’s oiliness can change over time and may also be influenced by factors such as stress, genetics, fluctuating hormones, and humidity. Identifying whether your skin is oily, normal, dry, sensitive, or combination can help you choose the right products to promote hydrated, smooth, and radiant-looking skin.

Combination skin

Combination skin has two or more different skin characteristics at the same time. The T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) is usually oily with enlarged pores and shine, while the cheeks range from normal to dry. You may experience occasional breakouts in oily areas and tightness or flakiness in dry areas. Weather changes, stress, hormones, and skincare products.

Characteristics

  • Mix of oily and dry areas
  • Usually oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin)
  • Normal or dry cheeks

How it feels

  • Oily in some spots
  • Dry or normal in others
  • Avoid harsh exfoliants or scrubs

Recommended care

  • Mix of oily and dry areas
  • Lightweight moisturizer
  • Spot treatment for oily areas

Normal skin

Normal skin is well-balanced, meaning it is neither too oily nor too dry. It has a smooth texture, small or barely visible pores, and an even tone with minimal imperfections. People with normal skin usually experience few sensitivities and maintain a healthy moisture balance throughout the day. This skin type tends to feel comfortable, soft, and hydrated, showing a natural glow without excess shine. Proper care can help maintain its balanced condition and protect it from environmental changes.

Characteristics

  • Balanced moisture and oil levels
  • Small or barely visible pores
  • Smooth, even skin texture

How it feels

  • Comfortable throughout the day
  • Not oily and not dry
  • Soft and smooth to the touch

Recommended care

  • Use a gentle cleanser to maintain balance
  • Apply a lightweight moisturizer daily
  • Use SPF 30+ every day to protect the skin

Sensitive Skin

Sensitive skin is more prone to irritation and reacts easily to environmental factors, harsh products, or changes in weather. It may appear red, itchy, or inflamed, and can feel tight or uncomfortable. This skin type requires gentle care and mild, soothing products to maintain balance and prevent flare-ups. Identifying triggers and following a simple, calming skincare routine can help sensitive skin remain healthy, hydrated, and comfortable.

Characteristics

  • Prone to redness, irritation, or inflammation
  • May feel tight or itchy
  • Easily reacts to products, weather, or stress

How it feels

  • Tight or uncomfortable after cleansing
  • Sensitive to hot water or harsh chemicals
  • Can sting, burn, or feel itchy easily

Recommended care

  • Use gentle, fragrance-free cleansers
  • Apply lightweight, soothing moisturizers
  • Avoid harsh exfoliants or scrubs

Dry skin

Dry skin lacks sufficient natural oils and moisture, making it feel tight, rough, or flaky. It can appear dull or ashy, and fine lines may be more noticeable. This skin type is more sensitive to harsh cleansers, cold weather, and low humidity. Proper care with hydrating products and gentle routines helps restore moisture, improve texture, and maintain a healthy, smooth appearance.

Characteristics

  • Lacks natural oils and moisture
  • Rough or flaky texture
  • Dull or ashy appearance

How it feels

  • Tight, especially after cleansing
  • Rough or uneven to the touch
  • Can feel itchy or uncomfortable

Recommended care

  • Use gentle, hydrating cleansers
  • Apply rich moisturizers daily
  • Avoid long hot showers that strip oils

Oily skin

Oily skin produces excess sebum, giving it a shiny or greasy appearance, especially in the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin). This skin type is more prone to clogged pores, blackheads, and acne breakouts. While it may appear more resilient and less prone to wrinkles, oily skin still requires a proper skincare routine to balance oil production, reduce shine, and maintain clear, healthy skin.

Characteristics

  • Produces excess sebum
  • Shiny or greasy appearance
  • Enlarged pores, especially in T-zone

How it feels

  • Oily or sticky throughout the day
  • May feel heavy after sweating
  • Can be prone to breakouts and blemishes

Recommended care

  • Use gentle, foaming cleansers twice daily
  • Apply oil-free or lightweight
  • Use clay masks occasionally

Acne skin

Acne-prone skin is more susceptible to breakouts, blackheads, whiteheads, and inflamed pimples. It can be oily, combination, or even dry, but the main issue is clogged pores and excess sebum that trigger acne formation. This skin type requires gentle, non-comedogenic products, proper cleansing, and treatments that reduce inflammation and prevent further breakouts while maintaining skin balance.

Characteristics

  • Enlarged or clogged pores
  • Redness or inflammation may occur
  • Prone to pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads

How it feels

  • Oily or sticky in areas prone to acne
  • Can feel inflamed or tender at spots
  • Sensitive to harsh products

Recommended care

  • Apply lightweight, oil-free
  • Use gentle, non-comedogenic cleansers twice daily
  • Use sunscreen suitable for acne-prone skin
